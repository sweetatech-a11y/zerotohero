Google Tag Manager — Setup Guide

How to set up Google Tag Manager & grant agency access
Your Logo Here

Google Tag Manager (GTM) lets us install and manage all your marketing tracking codes (Google Ads, Meta Pixel, Analytics, etc.) from one place — without needing to touch your website code every time. This guide walks you through creating a GTM account and giving us access. The process takes about 5–10 minutes.

📋 What You'll Need Before Starting

  • A Google account (Gmail or Google Workspace email)
  • Your website URL

Already have Google Tag Manager? If your website developer or a previous agency set up GTM, skip Part 1 and go directly to Part 2 to grant us access. If you're not sure whether you have GTM, ask your web developer or let us know — we can check for you.

Part 1 — Create Your Google Tag Manager Account
1

Go to Google Tag Manager

Open your browser and go to https://tagmanager.google.com

Sign in with your Google account.

2

Click "Create Account"

Click the "Create Account" button.

3

Set up your Account

Enter an Account Name (use your business name). Select your Country.

4

Set up your Container

Enter a Container Name (use your website URL, e.g., "www.yourbusiness.com"). Select "Web" as the target platform. Click "Create".

5

Accept the Terms of Service

Review and accept Google's Terms of Service when prompted.

6

Note your Container ID

You'll see a popup with installation code. Your Container ID starts with "GTM-" and appears at the top of the screen. Write this down — we'll need it.

You don't need to install the code yourself — we'll handle the installation, or we'll send the code snippets to your web developer with clear instructions.

Part 2 — Grant Us Access to Your Tag Manager
1

Go to the Admin panel

In Google Tag Manager, click the "Admin" tab at the top of the screen.

2

Open User Management

Under the "Account" column (left side), click "User Management".

3

Click the "+" button and select "Add users"

Click the blue "+" icon in the top-right corner, then click "Add users".

4

Enter our email address

Type in the email address we provide (shown in the green box below).

5

Set Account-level permissions

Check the box for "Administrator" at the Account level. This lets us manage container settings and user access.

6

Set Container-level permissions

For your website container, select "Publish" permission. This lets us create, edit, and publish tracking tags on your site.

7

Click "Invite"

Review the permissions and click "Invite". We'll receive an email and accept the invite on our end.
