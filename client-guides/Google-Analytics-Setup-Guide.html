Google Analytics 4 — Setup Guide

How to set up Google Analytics on your website & grant agency access
Your Logo Here

Google Analytics tracks how visitors find and use your website. This data is essential for measuring the performance of your marketing campaigns. This guide walks you through creating a Google Analytics 4 (GA4) property and giving us access. The process takes about 10 minutes.

📋 What You'll Need Before Starting

  • A Google account (Gmail or Google Workspace email)
  • Your website URL
  • Access to your website backend (or your web developer's contact info)

Already have Google Analytics? If you already have a GA4 property set up, skip Part 1 and go directly to Part 2 to grant us access.

Part 1 — Create Your Google Analytics 4 Property
1

Go to Google Analytics

Open your browser and go to https://analytics.google.com

Sign in with your Google account and click "Start measuring".

2

Create an Account

Enter an Account name — use your business name. Check the data sharing options (recommended to keep defaults). Click "Next".

3

Create a Property

Enter a Property name (e.g., "My Business Website"). Select your time zone and currency. Click "Next".

4

Enter your business details

Select your industry category and business size. Check the boxes for your business objectives (e.g., "Generate leads", "Drive online sales"). Click "Create".

5

Set up a Web data stream

Select "Web" as your platform. Enter your website URL and give the stream a name (e.g., "Main Website"). Click "Create stream".

You'll see a Measurement ID that starts with "G-" — write this down, we'll need it.

6

Don't worry about installing the tracking code

Google will show you installation instructions — you can skip this. We'll handle installing the tracking code on your website using Google Tag Manager as part of our setup process.

Part 2 — Grant Us Access to Your Analytics
1

Go to the Admin panel

In Google Analytics, click the gear icon (Admin) in the bottom-left corner of the screen.

2

Open Account Access Management

Under the "Account" column, click "Account Access Management".

3

Click the "+" button and select "Add users"

Click the blue "+" button in the top-right corner, then select "Add users".

4

Enter our email and set the role to "Editor"

Type in the email address we provide (shown in the green box below). Set the role to "Editor".

Editor allows us to create reports, set up goals, and configure your analytics — everything needed to measure your campaigns. You remain the Admin/Owner.

5

Click "Add"

Click "Add" to grant access. We'll now be able to view and configure your analytics data.
