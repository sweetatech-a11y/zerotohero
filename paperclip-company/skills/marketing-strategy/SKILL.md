# Marketing Strategy

This skill guides the Marketing Director in translating company-level goals into actionable department plans, making channel strategy decisions for each client, and managing cross-channel coherence across SEO and paid ads.

---

## 1. Goal Decomposition Framework

When the COO assigns a marketing goal, break it into executable tasks using this framework.

### Step 1: Classify the Goal

| Goal Type | Definition | Primary Agent |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Rank improvement | Move a keyword up X positions in Maps or Organic | SEO Specialist |
| Lead volume increase | Generate X more leads per month | Ads Specialist (primary) + SEO Specialist (support) |
| Lead quality improvement | Reduce CPL or increase appointment rate | Ads Specialist |
| Brand visibility | Increase GBP impressions, brand mentions | SEO Specialist (GBP posts) + Brand Monitor Specialist (monitoring) |
| Review/reputation improvement | Increase star rating or review count | Review Specialist |
| New campaign launch | Start advertising on a new platform or for a new service | Ads Specialist (requires board approval) |

### Step 2: Define a Measurable Outcome

Every task must have a measurable success criterion. Vague goals are not acceptable.

- ❌ "Improve SEO for Client A"
- ✅ "Rank Client A's keyword 'emergency plumber [City]' in the Google Maps 3-pack within 60 days"

### Step 3: Identify Dependencies

Before assigning, check for cross-department needs:
- Does this require a new GHL landing page or form? → Coordinate with CRM Director
- Does this require a new ad creative? → Queue creative brief for board approval before briefing Ads Specialist to launch
- Does this require reputation baseline data? → Check with Reputation Director

### Step 4: Assign with Context

When assigning a task to a specialist, include:
- The goal and success criterion
- The client name and relevant context
- Any constraints (budget limits, style guides, approval gates)
- The deadline for a deliverable back to you

## 2. Channel Strategy by Client Situation

Use this guide to recommend the right channel mix for each client based on their situation.

### Situation A: New Client, Starting from Scratch
- **Month 1 priority:** GBP setup + citation cleanup (SEO) + lead intake system (CRM). Do not spend on ads until the lead funnel is working.
- **Month 2:** Begin paid ads (small test budget $500–$1,000/month). Measure CPL.
- **Month 3+:** Scale what works. Increase ads budget if CPL is on target. Double down on SEO if ranking momentum is showing.

### Situation B: Client Has Budget, Needs Leads Fast
- **Primary channel:** Meta Ads + Google Search Ads — immediate lead flow, budget-dependent.
- **Parallel track:** GBP optimization and citation cleanup run simultaneously for long-term SEO foundation.
- **KPI:** CPL target established at kickoff, reviewed monthly.

### Situation C: Client is Already Running Ads, Wants Better Results
- **Immediate action:** Paid ads audit (assign to Ads Specialist).
- **Then:** Fix the worst issues (creative fatigue, broad targeting, low-quality audiences) before spending more.
- **Parallel:** Evaluate if SEO can offset some of the ad dependency over 3–6 months.

### Situation D: Client Wants SEO Only (No Ad Budget)
- **Focus:** GBP optimization + post cadence + citation cleanup + review volume growth.
- **Expectations:** Set clear timeline — meaningful rank movement takes 3–6 months. Educate the client.
- **Reporting:** Focus on GBP impressions and call clicks (leading indicators) before rankings fully move.

## 3. Monthly Marketing Sprint Planning

At the start of each month, produce a per-client marketing sprint plan and share it with the COO for the weekly digest.

### Sprint Plan Format

```
MARKETING SPRINT PLAN — [Client Name] — [Month]

GOAL THIS MONTH: [1–2 sentence goal in plain English]

SEO SPECIALIST TASKS:
1. [Task] — Deliverable: [what] — Due: [date]
2. [Task] — ...

ADS SPECIALIST TASKS:
1. [Task] — Deliverable: [what] — Due: [date]
2. [Task] — ...

PENDING APPROVALS THIS MONTH:
- [Any items that will need board approval before execution]

CROSS-DEPARTMENT DEPENDENCIES:
- [Any CRM, Reputation, or Reporting coordination needed]

SUCCESS METRIC:
[How you'll know this month was successful]
```

## 4. Cross-Channel Coordination

Local marketing works best when channels reinforce each other. Watch for these cross-channel opportunities and flag them to the relevant agents:

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Ads are generating high click volume for "[service] + [city]" keyword | Tell SEO Specialist to prioritize GBP posts and content targeting that same keyword |
| A GBP post about a specific service gets high engagement | Tell Ads Specialist: this service resonates — consider testing it as an ad angle |
| A negative review mentions a specific service quality issue | Tell Ads Specialist: pause any ads highlighting that service until the issue is resolved |
| Reputation score improves above 4.5 | Tell Ads Specialist: add social proof ("500+ 5-star reviews in [City]") to ad copy |
| Review volume is low | Coordinate with CRM Director to ensure the post-appointment review request workflow is active |

Document cross-channel coordination decisions in the monthly sprint plan so the COO has visibility.
